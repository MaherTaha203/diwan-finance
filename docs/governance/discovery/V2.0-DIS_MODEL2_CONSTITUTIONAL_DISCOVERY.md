## Section 1 · MODEL2 Activation

**SETTLED**
- MODEL2 is **defined and inert** (`model2.js:123`, `DEFINED_INERT_P2A`); its EVENTS already govern
  classification & treasury identity (FOC‑025). Runtime allocation is **pooled oldest‑first** (FOC‑023/025).
- The declared `ALLOCATION_ORDER` is `subscription_2025 → subscription_2026 → historical_debt → future_credit`
  (`model2.js:104‑109`) — **declared, not executed**.
- Engineering activation mechanics (feature flag, staged rollout, reversible migration, pre‑migration
  Supabase snapshot, full Lab re‑certification) are **already mandated** by the V1 Migration Strategy and
  are ADR‑003 engineering concerns — **not** owner business policy.

**OPEN → OD‑01** (see Decision Register): *Does the owner intend runtime allocation to switch from pooled
oldest‑first to the declared `ALLOCATION_ORDER`, and is that declared order the correct business intent?*

**Constitutional impact:** Laws 2 (Derivation), 3 (Single Source), 4 (Explicit Classification). If runtime
allocation changes, the derivation rule's *output* changes → a **constitutional amendment** documenting the
executed order is required.

---

## Section 2 · Settlement Policy

**SETTLED**
- **Overpayment → member credit** (outstanding closes first, remainder preserved as credit): fixed by the
  owner's FOC‑022 ruling and `fin.js:89‑90`.
- **Value conservation & derivation** of every allocation: Laws 1, 2.

**OPEN**
- **OD‑02 · Credit consumption order & trigger** — when a member holds a credit, *how* is it consumed
  against obligations (which order; automatic on dues generation vs manual)? Today it is absorbed
  automatically by pooled netting (`fin.js:89`); MODEL2's declared order puts `future_credit` last. These
  must be reconciled by explicit decision.
- **OD‑03 · Intra‑step tie‑breaking & historical vouchers** — within one allocation step (e.g., two
  obligations of the same year, or several historical items), what is the deterministic order, and do
  pre‑MODEL2 historical vouchers re‑allocate under the new order or remain as recorded (Law 5)?

## Section 3 · Historical Deficit (BO‑06)

**SETTLED**
- **Definition of the communal historical deficit** and its bound: Law 9 (reduce toward zero only; excess
  overflows to Food) — certified FOC‑006/007/022.
- **Inflows** to the deficit treasury (ق4 collection, directed donation, ق5 transfer) are defined and certified.
- The **`historical_deficit_settlement` event exists** (`model2.js:65`, outflow, decreases_deficit) but has
  **no live capture path and no policy** — BO‑06 is deferred (FOC‑012, owner‑excluded).

**OPEN → OD‑04 · BO‑06 settlement policy** — the entire outflow side is undefined: *who* are the historical
creditors, *from which treasury* a settlement pays, the *accounting entries*, and the *reporting behaviour*.
No part of this is derivable — it is pure business policy.

## Section 4 · Refund Policy

**SETTLED**
- The **`refund` event exists** (`model2.js:90`, `treasury:'FROM_LINKED_ORIGIN'`, reverses a linked receipt)
  but has **no live path and no policy** — Refund is reserved (FOC‑013, owner‑excluded).
- Today, reversing a receipt's effect is done by **cancellation** (BO‑03, preserved void — FOC‑019).
- **Closed‑period immutability** (Law 11) currently **blocks** any alteration of a voucher dated in a locked year.

**OPEN → OD‑05 · Refund policy** — every sub‑rule is undefined and needs owner decision: eligibility; full vs
partial; refund **after** the receipt already settled an obligation; refund **after year close** (direct
conflict with Law 11 — needs an explicit constitutional resolution); the **funding source** treasury; and the
required **audit trail**.

## Section 5 · Credit Balance Behaviour

**SETTLED**
- Credit is `−finalBalance` and is **automatically absorbed** by future dues under pooled netting (`fin.js:89`).

**OPEN**
- **OD‑06 · Credit lifecycle policy** — does a credit **expire** (and when)? Is future consumption **automatic
  or manual** (ties to OD‑02)? **Cross‑year** carry (settled: it carries, since balance persists — confirm)
  and **cross‑program** behaviour (e.g., can a food credit offset a diwan obligation? — SETTLED as **no** by
  Law 8 custody, unless the owner amends). Confirm or override.

## Section 6 · Accounting Integrity

**SETTLED (invariants MODEL2 must preserve — no decision needed, stated for the amendment package)**
- **Historical immutability** — Law 5 (full‑snapshot versions; history never rewritten) + Law 11 (closed period).
- **Reversals/corrections** — done as preserved edits/cancellations (BO‑02/03), never destructive.
- **Audit trail** — Law 6 (who/when/why) on every transition.
- **Constitutional invariants** — Laws 1 (conservation), 7 (atomicity), 8 (custody), 10 (split exactness),
  12 (identity uniqueness) hold across any MODEL2 change.

**OPEN:** none intrinsic — but OD‑04 and OD‑05 must each be shown to preserve these invariants (Agent B gate).

## Appendix A — Open decisions in the mandated 10‑part format

*(Per the phase's QUESTION FORMAT; one decision per block; related sub‑parts grouped, unrelated ones separated.)*

### OD‑01 · Activate the declared MODEL2 allocation order?
1. **Business context.** Payments are today allocated pooled oldest‑first; MODEL2 declares a fixed order
   (2025→2026→historical→future_credit) but does not execute it.
2. **Why required.** MODEL2 implementation cannot begin without knowing whether runtime should adopt the
   declared order or keep pooled oldest‑first.
3. **Affected laws.** 2 (Derivation), 3 (Single Source), 4 (Explicit Classification).
4. **Affected business rules.** Member payment allocation; credit formation; historical‑debt reduction.
5. **Implementation impact.** Activate the allocation engine behind a reversible flag; full Lab re‑certification.
6. **Accounting impact.** Could change *which* obligation a given payment reduces (not the total); credit
   timing may shift.
7. **Advantages.** (Activate) runtime matches the ratified model; explicit, auditable order. (Keep) zero change,
   already certified.
8. **Disadvantages.** (Activate) migration + re‑certification cost; possible per‑member statement diffs.
   (Keep) declared model stays inert (documented divergence persists).
9. **Recommended option.** Activate **only if** the declared order reflects true intent (confirm in OD‑02/03);
   otherwise amend the order first. No recommendation on the business preference itself.
10. **Consequences of postponing.** V2.0 cannot start; MODEL2 remains inert (acceptable — no harm).

### OD‑02 · Credit consumption — order and trigger
1. **Business context.** A member credit (overpayment) must be consumed against future obligations.
2. **Why required.** The declared order places `future_credit` last, but pooled netting consumes it as soon
   as new dues appear — these differ and must be reconciled.
3. **Affected laws.** 1 (Conservation), 2 (Derivation).
4. **Affected business rules.** Dues generation (BO‑10); member balance; credit lifecycle.
5. **Implementation impact.** Defines the allocation engine's credit step + whether consumption is automatic
   at dues‑generation or a manual action.
6. **Accounting impact.** Determines when a credit turns into a paid obligation on the statement.
7. **Advantages.** (Automatic) simplest, matches today. (Manual) explicit control per member.
8. **Disadvantages.** (Automatic) less control; (Manual) new UI + operational burden (contradicts single‑user simplicity).
9. **Recommended option.** Automatic at dues‑generation (matches current certified behaviour) unless the owner
   wants manual control.
10. **Consequences of postponing.** OD‑01 cannot finalize (credit step undefined).

### OD‑03 · Intra‑step tie‑break & historical vouchers
1. **Business context.** Within one step there may be several obligations; and pre‑MODEL2 vouchers exist.
2. **Why required.** Determinism (Law 2) and history preservation (Law 5) require an explicit rule.
3. **Affected laws.** 5 (Immutable History), 2 (Derivation).
4. **Affected business rules.** Allocation determinism; treatment of already‑recorded historical allocations.
5. **Implementation impact.** Sort key within a step; whether activation re‑allocates history or only new payments.
6. **Accounting impact.** Re‑allocating history would rewrite past statements (Law 5 risk); forward‑only avoids it.
7. **Advantages.** (Forward‑only) preserves history, safest. (Re‑allocate) full consistency with new order.
8. **Disadvantages.** (Forward‑only) old vs new coexist; (Re‑allocate) violates Law 5 unless amended.
9. **Recommended option.** **Forward‑only** (Law 5) — new order applies to payments after activation; history unchanged.
10. **Consequences of postponing.** OD‑01 cannot finalize.

### OD‑04 · BO‑06 historical‑deficit settlement policy
1. **Business context.** The communal historical deficit can receive money but has no defined way to *settle*
   its creditors (BO‑06 deferred).
2. **Why required.** F‑06 cannot be built without the settlement rules; FOC‑012 stays excluded until then.
3. **Affected laws.** 8 (Custody), 9 (Deficit Bounds).
4. **Affected business rules.** Who is a historical creditor; settlement amount limits; treasury outflow.
5. **Implementation impact.** New capture path (a certified BO‑06) + accounting entries + reporting.
6. **Accounting impact.** Outflow from the historical‑deficit treasury; deficit decreases; must respect Law 9
   (never create a surplus).
7. **Candidate options (for owner selection — NOT recommendations).**
   - **A · Pay‑as‑funded from the deficit treasury:** settle named creditors directly out of the
     historical‑deficit treasury as it accumulates; each settlement decreases the remaining deficit, capped so
     it never creates a surplus (Law 9). *Adv:* single source, Law‑9‑native, no new treasury. *Disadv:* limited
     to available deficit funds; needs a creditor registry.
   - **B · Earmarked reserve:** route a defined share (e.g., part of Food overflow) into a settlement reserve
     that pays creditors. *Adv:* predictable funding. *Disadv:* new treasury/reserve concept; more accounting.
   - **C · Tracked obligations:** record each creditor as a payable, settled opportunistically when funds allow.
     *Adv:* full traceability per creditor. *Disadv:* most complex; new ledger.
8. **Disadvantages** — captured per option above; all must preserve Laws 8 & 9.
9. **Recommended option.** **None** — settlement model is pure business policy; the discovery offers the
   candidates above for the owner to choose/modify but does not recommend one (never invent accounting behaviour).
10. **Consequences of postponing.** BO‑06 (F‑06) and FOC‑012 remain deferred (acceptable; current state).

### OD‑05 · Refund policy
1. **Business context.** No first‑class refund exists; reversal is done today by preserved cancellation.
2. **Why required.** F‑07 cannot be built without eligibility, partial rules, post‑settlement/post‑close
   handling, funding source, and audit.
3. **Affected laws.** 1 (Conservation), 5 (Immutable History), 8 (Custody), **11 (Locked Period)**.
4. **Affected business rules.** Eligibility; full/partial; refund after the receipt settled an obligation;
   refund after year close; source treasury.
5. **Implementation impact.** New capture path + linkage to the origin voucher (`FROM_LINKED_ORIGIN`).
6. **Accounting impact.** Outflow from the origin treasury; reverses a prior inflow; must conserve value and
   preserve history.
7. **Candidate options (for owner selection — NOT recommendations).**
   - **Eligibility:** (a) any linked receipt · (b) only receipts that have **not** yet settled an obligation.
   - **Amount:** (a) full only · (b) full or partial (currency‑preserving, per Law 10).
   - **Funding source:** (a) the origin treasury (`FROM_LINKED_ORIGIN`, per the event def) · (b) a refund reserve.
   - **After year close (Law 11):** (a) **forbid** — refunds only in an open period · (b) a **bounded amendment**
     that records the linked reversal in the *current* open period (history of the closed year untouched).
   *Adv/Disadv:* (any/partial/origin) most flexible but widest surface & audit burden; (unsettled‑only/full/
   open‑period‑only) safest, smallest constitutional change, closest to today's cancellation model.
8. **Disadvantages.** Refund **after year close** directly conflicts with Law 11 — option (a) preserves the law
   unchanged; option (b) requires a **narrow, explicit constitutional amendment** the owner must approve.
9. **Recommended option.** **None on eligibility/funding** (business policy). On the Law 11 conflict, the
   discovery flags two constitutional resolutions for the owner to choose: (a) refunds are only permitted in
   an open period; (b) a bounded amendment allowing a linked reversal recorded in the current period.
10. **Consequences of postponing.** Refund (F‑07) and FOC‑013 remain reserved (acceptable; current state).

### OD‑06 · Credit lifecycle (expiration / cross‑year / cross‑program)
1. **Business context.** Credits persist on the member statement indefinitely today.
2. **Why required.** Expiration and cross‑program rules change balances and reporting.
3. **Affected laws.** 8 (Custody), 1 (Conservation).
4. **Affected business rules.** Whether credit expires; cross‑year carry; cross‑program (food↔diwan) use.
5. **Implementation impact.** Expiry job/flag; cross‑program transfer path (if allowed).
6. **Accounting impact.** Expiry would remove value from a member (needs a destination — donation? forfeiture?).
7. **Advantages.** (No expiry) simplest, member‑fair. (Expiry) housekeeping.
8. **Disadvantages.** (Expiry) value must go somewhere → new accounting rule + owner policy; (Cross‑program)
   breaches Law 8 custody unless amended.
9. **Recommended option.** **No expiry; no cross‑program** (preserve Laws 1 & 8) unless the owner wants otherwise.
10. **Consequences of postponing.** OD‑01 credit step remains partly open (low risk — default is no‑expiry).

### OD‑07 · Edge‑case ratifications (E‑05/E‑06/E‑08)
1. **Business context.** Three edge cases depend on the above policies: refund‑in‑closed‑period, deactivated
   member holding credit, treasury shortage at settlement/refund.
2. **Why required.** "Nothing may remain undefined."
3. **Affected laws.** 8, 9, 11.
4. **Affected business rules.** Follow OD‑04/OD‑05/OD‑06 once decided.
5. **Implementation impact.** Each becomes a defined branch in the settlement/refund path.
6. **Accounting impact.** Determined by the parent decision.
7–8. **Advantages/disadvantages.** Inherited from OD‑04/05/06.
9. **Recommended option.** Resolve automatically once OD‑04/05/06 are decided; no separate policy if those
   are complete.
10. **Consequences of postponing.** These stay open until their parents are decided.
