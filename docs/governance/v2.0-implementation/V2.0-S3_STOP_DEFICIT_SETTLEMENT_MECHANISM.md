## 1 · What Slice 3 requires
Implement **BO‑06 — Settle Historical Deficit**: a certified outflow classified
`historical_deficit_settlement`, funded from the Historical Deficit Treasury, that **reduces the recorded
deficit toward zero** and **never creates a surplus** (Law 9), **capped at the remaining deficit**
(CA‑004 · ADR‑004 · OD‑04).

## 2 · What already exists (verified in code)
- The **event** `historical_deficit_settlement` is defined in `public/js/model2.js:65` with
  `treasury:'historical_deficit', cash:true, outflow:true, decreases_deficit:true`.
- The **read‑side treasury math** (`fin2.treasuryBalance`) already nets an outflow: a settlement row with
  `destination_treasury:'historical_deficit'` subtracts its amount from the deficit treasury cash.
- **Display aggregators** `fin2.deficitSettlements()` / `deficitSettlementTotal()` already list/sum settlement
  rows.
- The read‑time **Law‑9 clamp** exists: `composed().historical_deficit_remaining = min(0, opening + treasury)`,
  proven by `tests/constitutional-deficit-bound.cjs` (35/35) — but that proof sweeps **donation inflows only**
  and the test explicitly notes *"no settlement‑creation operation exists yet"* and that one would need its own
  proof.

## 3 · The exposed gap (the reason for the STOP)
The flag `decreases_deficit:true` is declared on the event but is **honored nowhere**. The remaining‑deficit
figure is computed as:

```
rem       = opening(−) + treasuryBalance('historical_deficit')      // opening is a FIXED negative constant
remaining = min(0, rem)                                             // Law‑9 read‑time clamp
```

Because a settlement is an **outflow**, `treasuryBalance` **subtracts** it, so `rem` becomes **more negative** —
the recorded deficit **grows**. That is the **opposite** of the frozen requirement ("a settlement reduces the
recorded deficit toward zero"). A settlement drawn from a treasury with insufficient earmarked cash would drive
the number further from zero.

**Root cause:** the opening deficit is a fixed configuration constant, and the frozen constitution does not
specify *how* a settlement outflow reconciles against that fixed opening in the remaining‑deficit formula.
Implementing BO‑06 as a plain outflow would corrupt the certified deficit figure; implementing it "correctly"
requires choosing an accounting mechanism the constitution has not defined. This is a genuine missing rule, not
a code defect — hence STOP rather than invent.

## 4 · The precise decision needed
When BO‑06 records a settlement of amount **X** (funded from the deficit treasury, capped so `X ≤ |remaining|`),
what is its **net effect on `historical_deficit_remaining`** (the certified figure), and on the Food‑overflow
rule?

| Option | Mechanism | Effect on remaining deficit | Effect on cash / Food overflow | Fidelity to frozen text |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **M‑A · Neutral (double‑entry)** | Settlement discharges X of the recorded obligation **and** pays X cash out of the deficit treasury. The remaining‑deficit formula **excludes** settlement outflows (obligation +X toward zero exactly offsets cash −X). | **Unchanged** (0) | Deficit‑treasury cash drops by X; the earmarked money that was collected is now actually paid out; no change to overflow. | Matches *"funded from the treasury"* + *"records the financial effect"*; treats "remaining deficit" as *still‑owed‑and‑unfunded*. |
| **M‑B · Reduce (literal)** | Settlement directly **lowers the recorded deficit** by X (remaining +X toward zero), capped so remaining never exceeds 0. | **Reduced by X** toward zero | Cash leaves; remaining‑deficit figure shrinks by X. | Most literal reading of *"reduces the recorded deficit toward zero"* + *"capped at the remaining deficit."* |
| **M‑C · Plain outflow** (naive/current math) | Settlement is a pure cash outflow; no special deficit handling. | **Increased** (grows more negative) | Cash leaves; deficit figure grows. | ❌ **Contradicts** the frozen text — rejected, listed only for completeness. |

The choice is **constitutional**, not stylistic: M‑A and M‑B produce **different certified deficit numbers** and
interact differently with the Food‑overflow rule and the future Liquidity Guard (ADR‑002 / OD‑07 E‑08 treasury
shortage). M‑C is unconstitutional.

## 5 · Recommendation (for the Owner's consideration — not a decision)
**M‑A (Neutral / double‑entry)** appears most consistent with the frozen wording taken as a whole: the deficit
treasury *holds earmarked money*, and a settlement is that money being *paid to the historical creditor* — the
obligation and the cash fall together, so the **still‑unfunded** remaining deficit is unchanged, while the audit
trail records the outflow (amount, date, actor, effect). It also keeps `constitutional-deficit-bound.cjs`
(inflow sweep) valid unchanged and needs only an additive settlement term in `composed()`.
**M‑B** is the more literal single‑phrase reading and is equally implementable; it changes the headline deficit
number on each settlement. The Owner's ruling decides which the certified figure must reflect.
